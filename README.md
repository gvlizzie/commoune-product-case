# The Commoune: A Structured Networking Platform for College Talent

## Overview

The Commoune is a database-driven networking platform designed specifically for tech students, alumni, and recent graduates within a given institution. Built initially for Oakwood University, it enables structured, mentor-to-mentee connections while supporting peer-to-peer networking through content sharing, messaging, and discovery tools. The system is designed as a scalable framework that can be adapted to other institutions.

## Problem

Tech students and early-career professionals often struggle to access relevant mentorship and career-aligned networks within their own institutions. Existing platforms are too broad and unstructured, making it difficult to find individuals with shared technical backgrounds, aligned goals, or actionable guidance in a localized ecosystem.

## Solution

The Commoune creates an organization-specific networking environment where students and alumni are matched based on technical skills, academic background, and career trajectory. The platform supports both mentorship relationships and open networking through a centralized content feed, direct messaging, and targeted search functionality, all within a defined institutional context.

## Core Product Loop

1. User creates a structured profile (skills, grad year, role, video intro)
2. System matches users based on shared technical background, goals, and institutional affiliation
3. User connects, messages, and engages with others or discovers content through the feed
4. Activity feeds back into discovery, visibility, and continued engagement

## What I Built

* Structured user profiles (skills, grad year, technical focus, video introduction)
* Mentor-mentee matching between students and alumni within the same institution
* Social feed for posting and engaging with content (LinkedIn-style)
* Direct messaging and connection request system
* Search and filtering for users and posts by skills, roles, and interests

## My Role

Led product definition, UX design, and system architecture. Built and deployed the platform using Base44 after pivoting from an initial Supabase and FlutterFlow approach to improve frontend performance and accelerate iteration.

## Key Decisions and Tradeoffs

* Switched tech stack mid-build from Supabase and FlutterFlow to Base44 to prioritize speed and usability
* Scoped the platform to a single institution to increase relevance and engagement before considering expansion
* Chose a PWA over a native application to reduce friction and increase accessibility
* Prioritized structured profile data and matching over open-ended networking to improve signal quality

## Use of AI

Used AI-assisted tools throughout development to accelerate prototyping, structure workflows, and support product decisions. Focused on increasing iteration speed and reducing time from idea to testable system.

## What I Learned

Institution-specific constraints can increase product relevance and engagement when designing early-stage platforms. Rapid iteration and usability matter more than perfect infrastructure, and clear structure drives stronger engagement than feature volume.
